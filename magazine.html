Social Work by CIMAGE Students:

Each year, CIMAGE students actively engage in initiatives to uplift the underprivileged through education. This time, they donated almirahs and carpets to Ved Vidhyalay students in Patna and distributed educational kits, including school bags, pens, and copies, to children in slum areas. Their dedication to social work reflects their belief that true knowledge extends beyond classrooms—it is about making a difference. By embodying the spirit of Goddess Saraswati, these students continue to serve as beacons of hope, fostering a brighter and more inclusive future through their selfless efforts.

Student’s Project Exhibiton:

On the occasion of Saraswati Puja, CIMAGE students presented the exhibition of various projects such as, apna bihar, mithila paintings, Chandrayan, Mahatma Buddha, Ram Mandir, Culture of India though Map, famous monuments, Black day pulwama attack, old Bihar and developed bihar, hand shake robot, android app, lightning robots etc. Students from various departments present their research projects, innovative ideas, and prototypes, reflecting their academic excellence and practical skills.CIMAGE Director Prof. Neeraj Agrawal and guests evaluate the projects, providing valuable feedback and recognition to the budding innovators.

Prize Distribution:

Saraswati Puja celebrations also provide a platform to celebrate and nurture talent and creativity beyond academics. Students who have showcased exceptional talent in music, dance, drama, painting, or any other form of creative expression are honoured during the prize distribution ceremony. By recognising and encouraging creativity, educational institutions foster a holistic approach to learning that values not only academic success but also artistic and cultural endeavours.

Cultural Performances by Students:

The Saraswati Puja celebration at CIMAGE featured mesmerizing student performances, showcasing their artistic talents. Students from all branches gathered at the Patliputra branch for a vibrant cultural program. The stage came alive with classical music, dance, and theatrical acts on themes like saving the girl child, women empowerment, and college life. Enthralling singing and dancing performances were dedicated to Maa Saraswati, while a Qawwali performance captivated the audience. This cultural extravaganza added creativity and entertainment, making the celebration truly memorable.
